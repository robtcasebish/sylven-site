Platform and process

Sylven is a health coordination platform. We connect patients with independent licensed Canadian physicians and Health Canada registered pharmacies. We do not employ physicians or dispense medication ourselves.

The process has three steps. You complete a clinical intake assessment online — typically five to ten minutes. An independent physician licensed in your province reviews your file and determines whether treatment is clinically appropriate. If approved, a prescription is sent to our pharmacy partner, who dispenses and ships your medication directly to you.

Sylven serves patients in all ten provinces and three territories. Physicians must be licensed in the province or territory where the patient lives. We match you with a physician who holds the appropriate license for your location.

Medication is shipped nationwide by our pharmacy partner. Delivery typically takes three to five business days regardless of where in Canada you are located.

All physicians on the Sylven network are independent, licensed Canadian medical doctors. They are not employees of Sylven. They are independent practitioners who have chosen to offer asynchronous and virtual assessments through our platform.

All treatment decisions are made solely by your physician. Sylven does not influence, direct, or override physician decisions. If a physician determines that treatment is not appropriate, that decision stands regardless of what the patient prefers.

Medication is dispensed by our Health Canada registered pharmacy partner and shipped directly to your door. Sylven does not handle, store, or ship medication. The pharmacy operates independently and is responsible for dispensing and delivery.

All medication arrives in plain, discreet packaging with no indication of contents on the outside. The billing name on your statement will show the pharmacy partner's name. Medication costs are paid directly to the pharmacy and are not collected by Sylven.

The intake assessment takes five to ten minutes. Physician review typically takes less than 24 hours. If approved, the prescription is sent to the pharmacy the same day. Medication ships within one to two business days of the prescription being received and arrives within three to five business days.

From the moment you complete your assessment to medication arriving at your door, most patients wait five to seven business days in total.

You receive a full refund of your program membership fee. No questions asked. Your physician will typically explain why treatment was not approved and may suggest alternative approaches or next steps.

A decision not to approve does not mean treatment will never be appropriate. It means the physician determined it is not appropriate based on your current health profile. You can discuss the decision with your physician through the patient portal.

Sylven is not a clinic. We are a health coordination platform. The distinction matters legally and practically. Sylven does not provide medical care. We provide the coordination infrastructure — the assessment tools, the patient portal, the pharmacy referral, and the support layer — that connects patients with independent practitioners.

The physicians on the platform conduct asynchronous assessments based on your submitted health information. This is different from a live telemedicine video call, though your physician may request a follow-up if they need more information before making a decision.

Your health information is protected under the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and applicable provincial health privacy legislation. It is shared only with your treating physician and dispensing pharmacist, both of whom are bound by their own professional privacy obligations.

Sylven does not sell, share, or use your health data for marketing purposes. All data is stored on Canadian servers. For full details, see our Privacy Policy.

Weight loss and GLP-1

Semaglutide is the active molecule in both Ozempic and Wegovy. In April 2026, Canada became the first G7 country to approve generic semaglutide, with Health Canada clearing products from Dr. Reddy's Laboratories and Apotex. The generics contain the identical molecule at the same doses as Ozempic.

One important nuance: the current generic approvals are for the type 2 diabetes indication, the same as Ozempic. Physicians already prescribe Ozempic off-label for weight management, and can do the same with approved generics. Wegovy, specifically approved for chronic weight management at 2.4mg, does not yet have a generic equivalent. More generic approvals, potentially including a weight management indication, are expected as Health Canada works through seven additional submissions. Pharmacy availability of existing approved generics is expected by late May 2026; initial supply may be limited while manufacturers scale production.

GLP-1 receptor agonists work by mimicking a hormone your body naturally produces after eating. They act on receptors in the brain that regulate appetite and satiety, reducing hunger signals. They also slow gastric emptying, which means food stays in your stomach longer and you feel full with less.

The result is a significant reduction in caloric intake without requiring the same level of conscious effort that conventional dieting demands. In clinical trials, patients lost an average of 15% of body weight over 68 weeks. GLP-1 medications also improve insulin sensitivity and have demonstrated cardiovascular benefits in patients with type 2 diabetes and obesity.

The most common side effects are nausea, mild constipation, and fatigue, particularly during the dose escalation period. These typically ease within a few weeks as your body adjusts. Your physician will prescribe a gradual titration schedule specifically to minimise discomfort during this period.

Less common but more serious side effects include acute pancreatitis and gallbladder disease. These are why a clinical assessment is required before prescribing, and why ongoing physician monitoring is part of the program. If you experience severe or persistent abdominal pain, you should contact your physician immediately.

GLP-1 therapy is generally considered a long-term treatment. Clinical research consistently shows that the majority of patients regain weight when medication is stopped. This is because obesity is a chronic metabolic condition, not a short-term problem with a fixed endpoint.

Most physicians recommend continuing treatment as long as it is effective and well-tolerated, alongside sustained lifestyle changes. Your physician will review your progress at regular intervals and help you plan an approach appropriate to your situation and goals.

Registered dietitian consultations are included with the weight loss program membership. A Canadian-registered dietitian provides personalised nutrition guidance based on your current eating patterns, health goals, and how you are responding to GLP-1 therapy.

The focus is on supporting muscle preservation during weight loss, managing the appetite changes that come with GLP-1 therapy, and building sustainable eating habits. GLP-1 medications are most effective when paired with appropriate nutritional guidance, which is why dietitian support is a core part of the program rather than an add-on.

The Canadian Adult Obesity Clinical Practice Guidelines indicate GLP-1 therapy for adults with a BMI of 30 or above, or a BMI of 27 or above with at least one weight-related comorbidity such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, sleep apnea, or cardiovascular disease.

If your BMI is below 27, you are unlikely to meet the current clinical criteria. Your physician will review your full health profile and make the final determination. If you are near the threshold and have qualifying conditions, we encourage you to complete the assessment anyway.

Men's health

Yes. Sildenafil is the active ingredient in Viagra. Health Canada requires generics to demonstrate bioequivalence, meaning the same molecule, the same absorption, and the same clinical effect. The only meaningful difference is cost.

Generic sildenafil is available in Canada at a fraction of the brand-name price, typically from $1 to $3 per dose versus $15 to $25 for branded Viagra. Both are manufactured under the same quality standards required by Health Canada.

Both are PDE5 inhibitors that work by the same mechanism — increasing blood flow during sexual stimulation. The main practical difference is duration. Sildenafil (Viagra) works for four to six hours and is typically taken as needed, about 30 to 60 minutes before activity.

Tadalafil (Cialis) lasts up to 36 hours and is available in both as-needed and low-dose daily formulations. Daily tadalafil means you do not need to plan around a dose, which some patients prefer. Your physician will discuss which option suits your situation.

Finasteride typically takes three to six months before visible results appear. It works by preventing further hair loss first, then allowing follicles that have been miniaturised to gradually recover. Most men see meaningful improvement at twelve months of consistent use.

The earlier treatment begins, the more effective it tends to be. Follicles that have been inactive for a long time may not recover as well as those that were recently affected. Stopping finasteride reverses its effect within six to twelve months, so it is generally continued long-term if it is working.

Erectile dysfunction can be an early indicator of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, or hormonal issues, because the vascular system and hormonal regulation that affect erectile function also affect broader health. Women's health

The concern comes from a 2002 study called the Women's Health Initiative, which led to a generation of women being denied effective treatment. That study used older oral synthetic hormones in older women, many of whom already had cardiovascular risk factors. The results were misapplied to all women, in all formulations, at all ages.

Twenty years of subsequent research, including re-analysis of the original WHI data, has substantially revised the picture. For healthy women in their 40s and 50s starting HRT within ten years of menopause onset, current evidence shows that the benefits generally outweigh the risks. The Canadian Menopause Society and the International Menopause Society now support HRT as appropriate first-line treatment for symptomatic women without contraindications. Your physician will review your individual situation and discuss the relevant evidence with you.

No. Perimenopause, the transition phase before menopause, can begin as early as the mid-40s and can last several years. Symptoms can be significant during this phase even while periods are still occurring, because hormone levels are fluctuating rather than simply declining.

You do not need to wait for twelve consecutive months without a period before seeking help. If your symptoms are affecting your quality of life, a physician can assess you and discuss treatment options appropriate to where you are in the transition. Starting treatment earlier often produces better long-term outcomes.

Women with an intact uterus require progesterone alongside estrogen to protect the uterine lining from abnormal growth. This is called combined HRT. Women who have had a hysterectomy can take estrogen alone, since there is no uterus to protect.

Formulation also matters. Estrogen delivered through the skin via a patch or gel avoids the first-pass metabolism of oral tablets and may carry a lower clot risk. Your physician will discuss which formulation is most appropriate based on your health history, preferences, and symptom profile.

Many women notice improvement in hot flashes and night sweats within two to four weeks. Sleep often improves shortly after. Mood and cognitive symptoms typically take six to eight weeks. Vaginal dryness and discomfort generally respond within one to three months.

Full response is usually assessed at three months, at which point your physician may adjust formulation or dose if needed. Some women require more than one adjustment before finding the right regimen. This is normal and expected with hormone therapy.

Billing and cancellation

The program membership fee covers non-insured support services: physician assessment and ongoing check-ins, patient portal access, care team messaging, prescription renewal coordination, and for the weight loss program, registered dietitian consultations.

Medication is a separate cost, paid directly to the pharmacy partner. Sylven does not mark up medication prices. The two-component structure means you always know what you are paying for, and you can see both costs clearly before committing.

The program membership fee covers non-insured services and is not covered by provincial health plans. Some private benefits plans or Health Spending Accounts (HSAs) may cover physician consultation fees or dietitian services. We recommend checking with your benefits provider directly.

Medication costs paid to the pharmacy may be partially or fully covered by private drug benefits plans. Generic medications are increasingly included on provincial formularies, though coverage for weight management indications varies by province and plan. Your pharmacy partner can provide an official receipt for benefits claims.

You can cancel at any time through your patient portal or by messaging your care team. There are no cancellation fees and no notice period required. Your membership will not renew after the current billing cycle ends.

If you are on a GLP-1 or HRT program, your physician will advise on how to discontinue medication safely before you cancel. Stopping GLP-1 or HRT abruptly without guidance is generally not recommended.

If your physician determines that treatment is not clinically appropriate for you, your program membership fee is refunded in full. This applies regardless of the reason for non-approval.

Medication costs are not refunded once dispensed, as is standard for prescription medication. For full details, see our Refund Policy.
