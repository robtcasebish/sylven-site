What we treat

Two conditions.
Both treatable.

Erectile dysfunction
is more common than
you probably think.

Erectile dysfunction affects roughly half of all men at some point. It becomes more common with age, stress, cardiovascular conditions, and metabolic factors. It is not a reflection of masculinity. It is a physiological response to identifiable causes.

PDE5 inhibitors such as sildenafil (Viagra) and tadalafil (Cialis) are among the most studied and effective medications in modern medicine. They work by increasing blood flow to the penis during sexual stimulation. For most men with ED, they are highly effective.

Your physician will assess your cardiovascular health, current medications, and the underlying cause before prescribing. ED can occasionally indicate an underlying condition that warrants attention on its own.

Male pattern hair loss
is hormonal, not
inevitable.

Male pattern baldness is caused by the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), which miniaturises hair follicles over time. It is genetic and hormonal. It is also, in many cases, stoppable.

Finasteride blocks the enzyme that converts testosterone to DHT, slowing or stopping hair loss in the majority of men who use it. Minoxidil increases blood flow to follicles and prolongs the growth phase of hair. Both have decades of clinical evidence behind them.

The earlier treatment begins, the more effective it is. Your physician will assess your health history and current medications before determining whether either medication is appropriate for you.

Who qualifies

Straightforward
eligibility.

Most healthy adult men qualify for ED or hair loss treatment. Your physician reviews your health history to confirm it is safe and appropriate for you specifically.

The assessment is free. If your physician determines treatment is not appropriate, you receive a full refund of your program fee.

Erectile Dysfunction

  • No active nitrate medication use
  • No recent major cardiac event

Hair Loss

  • Adult men 18 and over
  • Male pattern baldness (androgenic alopecia)
  • Liver conditions reviewed individually
