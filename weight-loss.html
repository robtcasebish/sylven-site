The science

What GLP-1
therapy actually does.

GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) receptor agonists are a class of medications that work with your body's own hormonal signals to reduce appetite, slow gastric emptying, and improve how your body regulates blood sugar and fat storage.

Semaglutide — the active ingredient in Ozempic and Wegovy — is the most clinically studied GLP-1 available. In clinical trials, patients lost an average of 15% of body weight over 68 weeks. For many, it is the first medication that has worked after years of other approaches.

It is not a shortcut. It is a clinically validated tool that works best alongside changes to nutrition and activity — which is why every Sylven program includes registered dietitian support.

Who qualifies

Clinical criteria
for GLP-1 therapy.

Eligibility is determined solely by your independent physician following a clinical assessment. The criteria below reflect current Health Canada and Canadian Adult Obesity Clinical Practice Guidelines.

Your assessment is free and takes five minutes. If your physician determines GLP-1 therapy is not appropriate, you receive a full refund of your program fee.

Check Your Eligibility
✓

BMI of 30 or above

Adults with a BMI at or above 30 typically qualify for GLP-1 weight management therapy under Health Canada guidelines.

✓

BMI of 27 or above with a qualifying condition

Adults with a BMI of 27–29 may qualify with at least one weight-related comorbidity — such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, sleep apnea, or cardiovascular disease.

✓

18 years of age or older

GLP-1 therapy for weight management is indicated for adults only. Sylven serves patients 18 and over.

—

Conditions requiring physician review

History of pancreatitis, medullary thyroid cancer, MEN2 syndrome, severe kidney disease, type 1 diabetes, or pregnancy may affect eligibility. Your physician assesses each case individually.
